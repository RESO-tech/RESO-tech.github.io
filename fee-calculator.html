He is free to choose any percentage of the Model Fee he wants to charge for the particular proceeding. The percentage charged can vary between 1% to 99%. However, if he chooses to charge his Fees as per the Act, he can mention 100% as his indicated Fee.

Q 2: Is there any ceiling on the percentage of Model Fee in some cases?
Ans: Yes, there is a ceiling for cases in which the amount in dispute is ₹ 5 lacs or less. In such cases, the DRP Fee cannot exceed more than 100% of Model Fee specified in the above table even if the Fee percentage mentioned by the DRP is more than 100%. This means that in cases where the claim amount is ₹ 5 lacs or less, the Fee percentage of the DRP cannot cross the Model Fee i.e. ₹ 45,000 and it will be automatically ceiled to 100%The Fee percentage higher than 100% of Model Fee indicated by the DRP will apply ONLY to cases where the amount in dispute is more than ₹ 5 lacs. If you still have doubts, you can write to us on www.reso.co.in or contact us at 40511111.

Q 3. What about any miscellaneous expenses, if any?
Ans: The Fee charged by the DRP will be inclusive of all the miscellaneous expenses. However, if there is an agreement between the parties and the DRP before the start of the proceedings mentioning any specific expenses to be borne by the parties, in that case, they will be bound with that agreement.

Q 4. If a DRP is willing to revise his percentage of the Model Fee for future cases. Can he do so? If yes, what will be the procedure?
Ans: Yes. A DRP can revise his percentage of the Model Fee for future cases to be applicable for next quarter with the prior permission of the Prompt Dispute Resolution Council (“Council”). The Council will approve the changes only if it feels that the reasons for the change in the percentage of model fee are justifiable.

Q 5. If a DRP is willing to change his percentage of the Model Fee in the existing cases, in the middle of the Proceedings, due to an increase in the number of hearings or complexity of the case. Can he do so? If yes, what will be the procedure?
Ans: Yes, if the same is MUTUALLY AGREED UPON among the DRP and related parties to the dispute. In such cases, the DRP shall inform in writing along with the consent of related parties, the revised fee to the Prompt Dispute Resolution Council (“Council”) with the date from which such change will be applicable. This changed fee is applicable for the mutually agreed case only.

Q 6 In case a DRP has to leave the Proceedings in the middle or even after 2 or 3 hearings, then how will he be compensated and what will be the procedure?
Ans: If a DRP has voluntarily absented himself for more than 3 hearings continuously, without the knowledge of the Council, then DRP will be removed from the case without any further compensation. However, in any other unexpected circumstance, if a DRP has to leave a particular case then he may mutually agree with related parties to the dispute on the amount of his compensation considering the no of hearings made by him. In case, no agreement could be reached among the parties and the DRP, the DRP can approach the Council to look into the matter. The Council, after considering facts and circumstances of each case including the advance amount received by the DRP up to the date, will announce its final decision which shall be binding on DRP and all other parties to the dispute.

Q 7. In case, there is more than one DRP for a single proceeding with different Fee percentages quoted by them, then how will they be compensated?
Ans: As already clarified on the Portal the parties can choose any DRP listed on the Portal based on the percentage of the Model Fee quoted by them. In a situation, where more than one DRPs are selected by the parties, then they will be compensated as per their percentage of the Model Fee quoted by each one of them by their respective parties.

Ans: Yes, the DRP’s i.e. the Mediator can charge the additional fees of ₹ 4,500 per hearing if the Mediation process is not concluded within the fi₹t six hearings. This means that the Mediator can charge ₹ 4,500 per hearing from seventh hearing onwards till the conclusion of the Mediation process.

Ans: Yes, there is a flooring for cases in which the amount in dispute is ₹ 50,000 or less. In such cases, the DRP Fee cannot be less than 100% of Model Fee specified in the above table even if the Fee percentage mentioned by the DRP is less than 100%. This means that in cases where the claim amount is ₹ 50,000 or less, the Fee percentage of the DRP cannot be less than the Model Fee i.e. ₹ 4,500 even if the Fee percentage mentioned by the DRP is for example 80% then it will be automatically floored to 100%.
